Website Evaluations

Good Website Design: SpaceX.com

The SpaceX website displays the incredible work that this company has done and the amazing science that they have enabled. The site is divided into 5 main pages (not including landing page), each describing one of SpaceX's endeavours / achievements. There's the Falcon9 and Falcon Heavy launch vehicles, the Dragon space capsule, their human spaceflight program, their rideshare program, and most importantly, Starship! Starship is the future of humanity in space. It is the spaceship that will hopefully allow humans to return to the moon for the first time in over 50 years! Plus, taking humans to Mars for the first time in human history!

  • Contrast:

    - With the primary color of the site being black and the foreground primarily being a smooth white color, a simply yet elegant contrast is created that's easy on the eyes and nice to look at. Coupled with foreground imagery of vibrant rocket plumes, everything about this site is awe-inspiring and will immediately capture your interest.

  • Repitition:

    - The repition of simplicity in every aspect of this site makes it not only easy to browse but easy to undestand as well. There is not a vast amount of different changing tones and colors, every part of the design is unified through the repeated color scheme.

  • Alignment:

    - Information is aligned in a way that conveys a large amount of knowledge in a neat, compact space. If you look for clutter and entropy on this site, you won't find any.

  • Proximity:

    - In association with the alignment of information, the proximity to their corresponding graphics again, conveys a lot of complex information and a quick and easy manner. They have also left plenty of space between information sections so as to make obvious when a reader is moving from one to another.

Bad Website Design: arngren.net

From what I can tell this site seems to be a foreign ebay-like site where users can buy and sell anything from smart phones to sports cars. It is difficult to discern accurate information about this site as it is in a different language.

  • Contrast:

    - The site certainly has a lot on contrasting colors. In fact, it has far too many contrasting colors out of order relative to the color wheel.

  • Repitition:

    - There is very little stylistic repition aside from squares and repulsiveness.

  • Alignment:

    - If the tires on your car were aligned like the items on this site, you would literally need Jesus to take the wheel.

  • Proximity:

    - The site doesn't seem to scale images and doesn't appear to have a good way of blocking items together into groups or at least some semblance of a pattern. Because as we all know, humans really love patterns... and squares (seriously, look around you right now. I bet a large number of the items around you are either square or rectangle shaped).
